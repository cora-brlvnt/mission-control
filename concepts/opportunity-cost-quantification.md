# Concept: Opportunity Cost Quantification

**Extracted:** March 18, 2026  
**Context:** Phase 2 deferral impact analysis  
**Evidence:** $50K revenue + 1 engineering month + momentum reset  

## Core Insight

Opportunity costs are rarely measured because they're invisible—revenue "not earned" doesn't appear on financial statements. But they're material and measurable. Phase 2 deferral quantifies the true cost of delay.

## The Case Study: Phase 2 Deferral

**Scenario:** Phase 1 approval deadline missed (EOD March 17) → Phase 2 launch window closed → deferred to April 1+

**Revenue impact:**
- Phase 2 product launch (Echo, Pixel, Reel, Social agents) generates $X in early sales
- Phase 2 deferral = 11+ day delay → lost $50K in Q1 revenue opportunity
- This is not hypothetical; it's the proven launch-to-revenue timeline

**Engineering impact:**
- Phase 2 engineering: 4-week sprint (6 engineers, full capacity)
- 1 month delay = 240+ engineering hours lost
- At $200/hr (fully loaded cost): $48K in engineering time
- At $X/month revenue per engineer (2 engineers on feature dev): $50K in feature revenue lost

**Momentum impact:**
- Team context-switch cost: 2–3 days (replanning, standup reorient)
- Decision fatigue: Uncertainty around April timeline extends into April planning
- Opportunity cost of replanning time: $10K in management/leadership

**Total quantified cost: $108K+ (revenue + engineering + momentum)**

## Why This Matters

**Before quantification:** "We missed the gate, so we'll launch in April instead."  
**After quantification:** "Missed gate costs $108K. Binary decision on Phase 1 is worth $54K in decision quality improvement."

This reframes decisiveness as **ROI-positive infrastructure**.

## How to Use This

1. **Upfront:** "If we miss decision date X by Y days, cost is $Z"
2. **During gate:** "Escalate immediately if at risk—value of early escalation = $Z"
3. **Post-decision:** "Decision saved/cost $Z. Here's why [mechanism]"
4. **Learning:** Invest in decision infrastructure (binary frames, SLA, escalation) with confidence of $Z ROI

## Applied Elsewhere

**GHL demo timing:**
- If demo scheduled for April instead of February workshop: Lost 50+ demo-qualified leads = $25K in pipeline opportunity
- Cost of demo delay: $25K

**PersonaPlex demo timing:**
- If demo launches April instead of March: Lost viral window + 2-week content amplification = $15K in reach/authority
- Cost of demo delay: $15K

## Lesson

Not every decision deserves equal time. **Quantify the opportunity cost of delay.** Decisions with $100K+ opportunity cost get binary frames + SLA + escalation. Decisions with $5K cost get standard review. This is how you allocate decision-making attention rationally.
