# Window-Based Execution Model

## Definition
A compressed time-gated decision framework that replaces vague deadlines with hard, mechanical escalation. Removes indefinite deferral by coupling a deadline to automatic consequence.

## Components
1. **Hard deadline** (e.g., April 3, 12:04 AM EDT)
2. **Binary frame** (approve / revise / defer)
3. **Transparent auto-consequence** (e.g., April 8 emergency re-plan auto-triggers)

## Mechanism
- Time-bounded decision gate (48 hours vs. 10+ days vague)
- Silent past deadline = auto-action (not continued waiting)
- Consequence is predetermined, not surprise
- Removes pressure escalation (hard deadline is structural, not emotional)

## Evidence (April 1–3, 2026)
- **Result:** 48-hour window (April 1–3) → hard deadline → auto-trigger (April 8 re-plan)
- **No decision signal** by April 3, 12:04 AM → automatic consequence
- **Clarity:** Moved from indefinite "waiting" to "decision made or auto-deferred"

## Why It Works
- Pressure/escalation assumes communication gap; window-based assumes decision friction
- Hard deadline removes ambiguity ("is this still open?")
- Mechanical consequence removes human judgment (fair, predictable)
- Binary frame (3 choices) prevents vague "I'll think about it"

## Application
- **Phase/roadmap decisions** (approve/revise/defer options)
- **Quarterly re-planning** (hard date for strategy review)
- **Priority conflicts** (deadline forces tradeoff clarity)

## Contrast: Vague Deadlines
- "By end of Q2" = 90+ days, no checkpoints, indefinite wait
- "March 27 with T+24h escalation + April 1 auto-escalation + April 3 hard deadline" = clarity at every step

## Outcome
Converts "decision pending" (friction, silent cost) to "decision made or consequence applied" (clarity, measurable).
