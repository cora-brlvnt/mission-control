# Decision Velocity as Bottleneck

**Date created:** March 6, 2026  
**Category:** Operations & process improvement  
**Key pattern:** Build speed >> Decision speed  

---

## The Problem

Phase 1 was completed in hours (Feb 23). Approval feedback is now **120+ hours overdue** (5 days past pre-agreed EOD Monday deadline).

This reveals a systemic pattern:
- **Build capacity:** Hours (fast)
- **Decision capacity:** Days (slow)
- **Gap:** 3–5 day approval delays on single-person decisions

---

## Why It Happens

1. **Open-ended requests** — "Any feedback on Phase 1?" creates ambiguity, delays response
2. **Context switching** — Decision-maker juggling multiple priorities; delivery sits in queue
3. **Implicit approval** — Stakeholder satisfied but doesn't formalize closure
4. **Scope mismatch** — Deliverable doesn't match expectations; feedback pending clarification

---

## The Cost

- Phase 1 delivered on time (Feb 23) ✅
- Phase 2 blocked from starting (still waiting for Phase 1 approval)
- Momentum lost: 120+ hours of idle time
- Project timeline at risk: Phase 2 target launch March 11–18 depends on approval *this week*

**Lesson:** Fast delivery doesn't matter if approvals are slow. Velocity is limited by the slowest decision-maker, not the fastest builder.

---

## Solution Pattern

**Use a 2-option decision frame instead of open-ended questions:**

```
INSTEAD OF: "Do you have feedback on Phase 1?"
USE: "Phase 1 ready for Phase 2 launch. Options:
  ✅ Approve & proceed (1-line response)
  🔄 Revise X, Y, Z (specific feedback)"
```

**Why this works:**
- **Clarity:** Binary choice removes ambiguity
- **Speed:** Reduces cognitive load (not open-ended)
- **Respect:** Acknowledges time constraints
- **Unblocks:** Enables next phase immediately

---

## Prevention Strategy

### For future projects:
1. **Pre-agreed decision deadlines** — Written confirmation: "Feedback by EOD Monday"
2. **Structured decision frames** — Always use binary or 3-option frames, never open-ended
3. **Escalation triggers** — +24h past deadline = gentle escalation with decision frame
4. **Explicit closure** — Require written approval before starting next phase

### Example workflow:
```
Phase 1 delivered (Monday 2 PM)
↓
"Feedback by EOD Tuesday" (pre-agreed)
↓
(If no response by EOD Tuesday)
Escalation: "Phase 1 ready for Phase 2. Options: A) Approve, B) Revise [X]. Reply by 10 AM Wed?"
↓
Response received
↓
Phase 2 starts immediately
```

---

## Key Insight

**Decisions are as much a deliverable as code.**

In fast-moving projects:
- Build speed is easy (good engineers can iterate quickly)
- Decision speed is hard (depends on single stakeholder, competing priorities)
- The gap between these two creates bottlenecks

Protect decision velocity the same way you protect build velocity: structured processes, clear deadlines, escalation triggers.

---

## Current Application

**Phase 1 escalation (March 6):**
- Send 2-option decision frame with pre-agreed response deadline
- Estimate Phase 2 timeline (7–10 days) conditional on approval
- Provide unblocking action: one-line approval = Phase 2 starts same day

---

## Broader Implications

This pattern likely repeats in other approval processes:
- GHL workflow deployment (awaiting workshop timing decision)
- PersonaPlex demo scheduling (awaiting date confirmation)
- Any multi-person workflow with external dependencies

**Prevention:** Apply 2-option decision frames + escalation triggers across all approval gates.

**Benefit:** Predictable velocity, reduced idle time, faster project delivery.
