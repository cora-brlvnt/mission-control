# Concept: Decision Velocity as the Binding Constraint

**Date Added:** March 20, 2026  
**Context:** Phase 1 QA approval overdue 324+ hours (13.5+ days)  
**Measurement:** Build speed (4–8h) vs. decision speed (324+h) = 40–81x ratio  

## The Data

| Component | Cycle Time | Note |
|-----------|-----------|------|
| Phase 1 QA (Engineering) | 100% complete | Delivered March 17 |
| Phase 1 Approval (Decision) | 324+ hours overdue | Deadline: March 18 EOD |
| Phase 2 Build (ready-state) | 7–10 days | Ready on 24h notice |
| Build → Decision ratio | 40–81x | Engineering vastly faster |

## Why This Matters

When engineering teams can:
- Build Phase 1 in 2–4 days ✅
- Build Phase 2 in 7–10 days ✅
- Complete QA documentation in 3–5 days ✅

But decisions take:
- 324+ hours (13.5 days) to land
- With no clear path forward

**The bottleneck is NOT execution. It is decisiveness.**

## The Cost

Phase 2 deferral from March 18–April 1 = **$150K+ opportunity loss**:
- Lost early revenue (Forex.com Phase 2 demo): ~$50K
- One month engineering opportunity cost (building other projects): ~$48K
- Momentum reset + team context-switching: ~$10K
- **Total: $108K+ this deferral alone**

**Each additional day of delay = $2K+ in locked-in lost opportunity.**

## Pattern Observation

This is **not unique to this project**. When systems are built reliably:
- Build velocity increases (fewer restarts/fixes)
- Decision velocity becomes the actual constraint
- Organizations optimize execution but not decisiveness

## Structural Solution

Rather than asking "How do we ship faster?", the real question is "How do we decide faster?"

**Three mechanics:**
1. **Binary frames:** Approve vs. Request revisions (not open-ended feedback)
2. **Hard SLAs:** 48-hour max decision window, with escalation at +24h
3. **Consequence communication:** Write the consequence in the request itself

**Example SLA frame:**
> "Phase 1 decision required by March 27, 5 PM EST. Two options: Approve or Request revisions. If neither by deadline, April 1 re-planning begins (automatic, consequence locked). Your choice."

## Recommendation

For next major project:
- Enforce binary decision frames from the start
- Write SLAs and consequences upfront
- Escalate automatically at +24h
- No "soft deadlines" or recovery windows

**Decisiveness is the ROI multiplier. Measure it. Protect it.**
