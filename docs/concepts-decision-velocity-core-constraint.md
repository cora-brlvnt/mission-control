## Root Cause Analysis

### 1. Open-Ended Feedback Without Binary Frames
- Original feedback: "Needs review," "Let's discuss further"
- No clear path to decision
- Ambiguity enables indefinite deliberation

### 2. No SLA Enforcement
- 48-hour SLA set March 9 → ignored (deadline March 10)
- No urgency signal
- No accountability for decision timing

### 3. Lack of Clear Options
- Before binary frame: unclear what "approval" means
- After binary frame: clear (Approve Phase 1 now OR revise later)
- Binary framing reduced friction; didn't eliminate delay

## What Works

### Binary Decision Frames
When applied (March 9), binary frames reduce ambiguity:
- **Option A:** Approve Phase 1 + launch Phase 2 (March 26–30 target)
- **Option B:** Revise Phase 1 further + defer Phase 2 (April timeline)

Clear options; faster decisions possible.

### SLA Enforcement
When enforced, SLA creates urgency:
- 48-hour windows force prioritization
- Decision gates clarify resource allocation
- No SLA = drift (as seen here)

## Lesson: Decision Speed > Engineering Speed

When infrastructure is solid and engineering is ready, **decision velocity becomes the differentiator**. Faster decisions ship more features, faster.

In this case:
- Infrastructure: Production-ready (28 days proven) ✅
- Engineering: Ready (Phase 2 fully scoped) ✅
- Decision: Bottleneck (258+ hours and counting) ❌

**The constraint that matters is the one you can't delegate.** Engineering can move fast; decisions require clarity at a higher level.

---

## Recommendation Going Forward

1. **Binary frames for all major gates** — Reduces ambiguity; enables faster decisions
2. **SLA enforcement on decision timing** — Creates urgency signal; prevents drift
3. **2–3 day lead time before sprint gates** — Allows decision propagation + team alignment
4. **Weekly decision checkpoints** — Clear cadence for deliberation vs. execution
