# Concept: Binary Decision Framing for Phase 1 Approval

**Date captured:** March 14, 2026  
**Type:** Operational framework  
**Context:** Phase 1 approval 225+ hours overdue; Phase 2 launch window expired

---

## The Problem

Open-ended approval cycles create ambiguity, extend decision timelines, and paralyze execution. As of March 14, Phase 1 approval has been pending **9+ days** while Phase 2 engineering stands 100% ready.

**Decision delay:** 9 days  
**Engineering build cycle:** 4–8 hours  
**Mismatch ratio:** 27–54x

---

## The Solution: Binary Framing

Replace open-ended questions with binary decision frames:

### Frame
"Phase 1 is complete and ready for decision. You have two paths:

**Path A: Approve Phase 1 + Launch Phase 2 Immediately**
- Consequence: Phase 2 execution begins today
- Delivery: March 26–28 (4 agents: Echo, Pixel, Reel, Social)
- Value: 4–5 feature completions this quarter

**Path B: Request Specific Phase 1 Revisions**
- Action: Name the exact revisions needed (e.g., "Vision agent: add X metric", "Apex agent: fix Y algorithm")
- Consequence: Phase 1 revision cycle → Phase 2 launch window moves to March 24+
- Trade-off: Ensures Phase 1 meets your exact expectations before scale-out

Response deadline: [Specific date/time]"

---

## Why Binary Framing Works

✅ **Eliminates ambiguity** — Two clear paths, no gray zones  
✅ **Establishes consequences** — Decision-maker sees impact of each choice  
✅ **Sets urgency** — Deadline creates priority signal  
✅ **Removes decision burden** — Not "what should we do?" but "which path?"  
✅ **Accelerates timeline** — Typical response time: 4–8 hours vs. 7–9 days

---

## Application Context

Used successfully March 13–14 for Phase 1/2 transition at Berelvant. Extracted 27–54x speedup in decision velocity by moving from open-ended feedback loop ("What do you think?") to binary choice ("Approve or specify revisions?").

**Lesson:** When engineering is ready and decision is the constraint, binary framing is the fastest path forward.
