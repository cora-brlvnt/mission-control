# Decision Velocity Bottleneck: Build Hours vs Decision Days

**Concept:** The gap between execution speed and decision-making speed as a strategic bottleneck.

**Context:** Phase 1 marketing agents delivered Feb 23 (built in hours); QA feedback now 120+ hours overdue (feedback stuck in queue).

---

## The Pattern

| Dimension | Speed | Example |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| **Build speed** | Hours | Phase 1 framework built + delivered in <24h |
| **Decision speed** | Days+ | Feedback stuck 120+ hours past agreed deadline |
| **Bottleneck effect** | 5+ days delayed | Blocks Phase 2 Echo agent launch (ready to start) |

---

## Root Causes (Diagnosed)

1. **Feedback stuck in queue** — Not rejected, just pending
2. **Scope mismatch** — Expectations unclear; deliverable differs from vision
3. **Cognitive load** — Approval decision competing with higher-priority work
4. **Implicit approval** — Satisfied but didn't formally close ("looks good" ≠ "approved, proceed")

---

## Solution Pattern: 2-Option Decision Frame

Instead of open-ended questions:  
❌ "What do you think of Phase 1? Ready to move on?"

Use binary decision frames:  
✅ "Option A: Phase 1 approved, proceed with Phase 2 (7–10 day sprint, launch March 11–18)"  
✅ "Option B: Need revisions on X, Y, Z. Feedback: [specific notes]"

**Why it works:**
- Clarity: No ambiguity about options
- Reduced cognitive load: Binary choice < open-ended eval
- Faster closure: One-line decision vs multi-turn loop
- Momentum preserved: Unblocks entire Phase 2 upon approval

---

## Best Practice for Future

**Escalation trigger:** +24 hours past agreed deadline

**Action:** Send gentle status reminder:
- Acknowledge time constraints
- Provide 2-option decision frame
- Estimated timeline for next phase upon approval
- One-line decision request

**Result:** Prevents 5+ day delays on critical path items

---

## Operational Implication

Build speed (hours) >> Decision speed (days) = **decisions are the actual bottleneck, not execution**.

Future workflows should:
1. Scope decisions upfront with clear approval criteria
2. Use binary frames instead of open-ended questions
3. Escalate at +24h past deadline with options
4. Treat decision bottlenecks as seriously as technical debt
