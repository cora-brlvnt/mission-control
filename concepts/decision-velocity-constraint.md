# Decision Velocity as Primary Constraint

**Date:** March 14, 2026  
**Pattern:** Identified and quantified

## The Mismatch
- **Build cycle:** 4–8 hours per agent feature
- **Approval cycle:** 9+ days (current Phase 1 delay: 225+ hours)
- **Velocity gap:** 27–54x mismatch

## Current Blocker (Phase 1)
- Phase 1 QA delivered: Feb 22
- Approval status: Pending feedback for 225+ hours (9+ days)
- Phase 2 engineering: Ready since March 10, idle pending Phase 1 decision
- Launch window: Expired EOD March 13; next window March 24+

## Root Cause
Open-ended feedback loops create ambiguity. Binary decision frames eliminate delays.

## Implication
**Problem framing > execution speed.** We can build 4 agents in 4 days; we cannot accelerate a 9-day approval delay with faster engineering.

## Solution Framework
1. **Binary options** — Approve OR specify exact revisions (not "feedback")
2. **Clear SLA** — Decision deadline (same day or next morning, not "when ready")
3. **Full impact disclosure** — What happens if delayed (next launch window, capacity cost)
4. **Ownership** — Single decision-maker, not committee feedback

## Lesson
Speed multiplies capacity. One hour of clear decision-making unblocks a week of engineering output.
