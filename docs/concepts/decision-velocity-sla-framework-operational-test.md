## The SLA Framework: Hard Deadlines + Consequences

Previously, soft escalations ("let's talk about this next week") didn't work. Week 5 demonstrated this: Phase 2 launch window (March 6–13) permanently expired when Phase 1 decision missed EOD March 17 deadline.

**Operational protocol (now live):**

1. **Hard deadline:** March 27 EOD (binary decision gate)
2. **Choice frame:** Approve Phase 2 sprint OR specify Phase 1 revisions [exact list]
3. **Automatic consequence:** If no response by March 27 EOD, April 1 full re-plan cycle activates
4. **Consequence communication:** Made clear upfront (no surprise escalation)

**Rationale:**
- Hard deadlines remove ambiguity (no "later this week" recovery window)
- Binary choice eliminates open-ended discussion loops
- Automatic consequence shifts incentives from "delay is safe" to "delay is costly"
- Consequence communication upfront ensures decision-maker understands SLA stakes

---

## Why SLA Works (Operationally)

**Soft escalations fail:**
- "Let's discuss next week" → no urgency, decision defers
- "This is important" → acknowledged but not urgent
- Open-ended timelines → natural tendency toward inaction

**Hard SLA works:**
- Clear end-date (March 27 EOD, not "by Friday")
- Quantified consequence (April 1 re-plan is material, non-trivial work disruption)
- Binary choice (removes negotiation loop; forces yes/no/revise)
- Consequence is **automatic**, not conditional on escalation

**Week 6 test:** March 27 will operationally validate whether hard SLA framework compresses decision velocity by 5–10x (from 45–95x mismatch back to 2–5x).

## Structural Insight: Decision Velocity vs. Capability

This framework reveals a crucial business insight:

**Bottleneck != Capability**

The limiting factor on Phase 1→Phase 2→execution pipeline is not:
- Engineering capacity (proven 4–8 hour delivery window)
- Technical risk (all architectures specified, tested, documented)
- Market risk (Renzo's authority foundation established, demand signal clear)

The limiting factor is:
- **Decision speed**
- **Approval velocity**
- **Choice architecture** (how decisions are framed and forced)

**Strategic implication:** Future success depends more on decision-making infrastructure than technical infrastructure. The hard SLA framework is testing whether we can build decision-making velocity to match execution velocity.

## Framework Applicability (Future)

This SLA model is repeatable for all future decisions requiring execution windows:

1. **Define hard deadline** (not "soon" or "by EOW", but specific date + time)
2. **Frame binary choice** (Approve option A, request revisions to option B, or choose option C)
3. **Announce consequence** upfront (what happens if deadline missed)
4. **Make consequence automatic** (not conditional on escalation; system triggers consequence on deadline pass)
5. **Measure outcome** (did hard deadline compress decision velocity to acceptable range?)

**Expected decision velocity improvement:** 5–10x compression (from 45–95x mismatch toward 2–5x acceptable range).
